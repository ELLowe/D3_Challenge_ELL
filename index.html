U.S. Census Bureau Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System 2014 Survey Data

Statistically Significant Correlation Discovered in U.S. Population: Living in Poverty and Lacking Healthcare

As the data appears, there seems to be a moderate positive correlation between the percent of residents living in poverty and the overall lack of access to healthcare per US state. Calculating a Pearson correlation coefficient produces a result of 0.52. Considering the survey is of demographic data, a value above 0.45 can be considered moderate. To affirm the correlation, performing a t-test produces a p-value of 0.00189. For a t-test, statistical significance can be attributed when the p-value is below 0.05. As such, the relationship between U.S. population living in poverty and access to healthcare seem significant.

Other correlated relationships found to be significant were:

Smoking and Obesity levels (Pearson Coeff: 0.74, p-value: 5.209e-29)

Smoking and Poverty levels (Pearson Coeff: 0.52, p-value: 2.378e-7)

Poverty and Obesity levels (Pearson Coeff: 0.46, p-value: 1.498e-40)

Poverty and Income levels (Pearson Coeff: -0.77, p-value: 1.829e-65).
